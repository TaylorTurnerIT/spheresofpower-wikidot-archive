[[toc]]

+++ What is a Martial Package?
A Martial Package is a collection of martial talents, including a Martial Tradition, that can be quickly applied to NPCs or monsters to convert them into a Practitioner. Martial Packages are extremely similar to templates, but cannot be taken by player characters. (The player version is just getting martial talent progression in the first place.)

If a Martial Package grants proficiency in any weapon(s), armor(s), or shield(s), a creature that gains the Martial Package may also obtain any or all of the equipment it gains proficiency in.

++++ Building A Martial Package
A Martial Package consists of a Martial Tradition and 20 martial talents numbered and ordered. When a creature gains a Martial Package, it gets the talents up to its Base Attack Bonus. For example, a creature with a BAB of +7 gets the first seven talents in the martial package. If its BAB increases (usually from leveling up or gaining additional hit dice), it gains new talents up to its BAB. Similarly, any loss of BAB reduces the talents from the Martial Package that a creature knows.

Each Martial Package should be thematic and designed to create a particular combat style. We particularly encourage basing ability progression on the basic Martial Tradition used to start it. For example, a Martial Package based on the Drunken Brawler tradition should probably focus on the Barroom and Wresteling spheres. Martial Packages can dip into spheres outside those it starts with, and when to do so is ultimately a matter of design judgement. Remember, Martial Packages are meant for enemies and monsters, not players - as a design principle, they should be fairly straightforward and have no more than two choices for any given effect. We don't want to slow things down by adding too many moving parts!

Martial Packages also provide a general description of the package and how it's intended to be used in play at higher levels. These should always be written for GMs and explain things like an attack routine and what effects are meant to stack with each other.
